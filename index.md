I am a PhD candidate in low-energy nuclear physics at the Ohio State University. My focus is primarily on building emulators and neural networks for nucleon interaction problems.

My PHD thesis focuses on developing reduced-order models for fast & accurate emulation of NN observables. There are different ways of accomplishing this, but it ultimately depends on what reduction scheme you choose. My main focus has been on developing model-driven (intrusive) projection-based approaches to predict neutron-proton (np) scattering observables. Another part of my research is applying data-driven (non-intrusive) approaches, such as feed-forward artificial neural networks, to the No Core Shell Model (NCSM) ab initio method in order to predict ground-state binding energies and radii. These codes were written using Python and are available under ''Software''.

My Master's thesis focused on analyzing the probabilities of cooper pairs tunneling through Josephson junctions with an inhomogeneous magnetic medium. This was done by applying the Bogoliubov-Valatin transformations in the ballistic (anomaly-free) regime for a tight-binding Hamiltionian and solving the resulting Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations (eigenvalue problem). The results were then compared to previous results obtained in the diffusive (anomaly-ridden) regime. These codes were written in Modern Fortran.

The purpose of this page is to keep all my publications, presentations, and projects in one place. Feel free to browse around.
